The Simple Economics of Machine Intelligence
hbr.org

What “digital communication” did for reducing the costs of search and communication (and the economic boom that followed), this article argues “machine intelligence” will do for reducing the costs of prediction, thereby triggering another major economic boom.

“The first effect of machine intelligence will be to lower the cost of goods and services that rely on prediction.” This will lead to using prediction to perform tasks that never used to rely on it, and importantly, the value of those activities and services that complement prediction will rise.

As such, “lots of tasks will be reframed as prediction problems”. Indeed.

Decoding The Thought Vector
gabgoh.github.io

Neural nets can be broadly understood as a series of transformations which process data into increasingly abstruse vectors of representations, believed to represent the data at different layers of abstraction. However the vectors themselves have thus far defied interpretation. The author argues that “we shouldn’t take these vectors literally, but rather as an encoding for a simpler, sparse data structure”, and then proceeds to use this idea to reverse engineer these data structures to assist in decoding the vector’s meaning. Pretty cool interactive results to play with too!
