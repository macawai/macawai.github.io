Reverse engineering a $500m AI company in a week
medium.com

“Nowadays everyone can take advantage of Artificial Intelligence without spending years and tons of money in R&D”. Putting this to the test, the author picked oPower, a smart meter engergy consumption company (acquired by Oracle), and reverse-engineered their “consumption pattern and recommendation/offer matching” features using off the shelf, open source frameworks and models, with a roughly appropriate public dataset. Result? Well, a pretty decent approximation of the value prop oPower was selling. K-mean clustering may not be “classic AI”, but hey, what is these days right?
