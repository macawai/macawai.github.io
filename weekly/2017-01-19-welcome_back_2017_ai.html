Featured

Game Theory reveals the Future of Deep Learning
medium.com

Decision making in the context of imperfect knowledge: sounds a lot like sitting in a holding cell wondering if your partner is going to rat on you. And now, adversarial networks are leveraging game theory to make predictions about the unknown. A rather pithy quote points out the meta-ness, “In our quest of understanding learning machines we end up with mathematics that were meant for the study of the interactions of intelligent beings”. Awesome.

And check out the research paper on “Counter Factual Reasoning and Deep Learning”.

Microsoft’s Nadella Warns Against ‘Hubris’ Amid AI Growth
bloomberg.com

Nadella, and by extension Microsoft, are playing the “lets ‘democratise’ AI to empower people” game rather than the “lets replace all the humans” version. This is a lovely idea, and somewhat oppositional to the broader consensus. Time will tell (if they mean it, and if it works). The title is slightly misleading however, as the hubris referred to is in relation to being overconfident in the picking of trends and the myopic focus on one product rather than allowing an evolution to take place.
