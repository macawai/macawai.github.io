Deep Learning without Backpropagation - DeepMind's Synthetic Gradients
iamtrask.github.io

A pretty cool hands-on prototype DeepMind’s recently proposed Decoupled Neural Interfaces Using Synthetic Gradients paper. The basic idea is to bypass backpropagation by having individual layers make “best guesses” (otherwise known as “synthetic gradients”) for what the data will say, and update their weights according to this guess. The upshot is that individual layers can mostly learn in isolation, increasing speed of training. This article looks at how to use and generate synthetic gradients as well as setting up a NN to play with the real thing. Enjoy!
