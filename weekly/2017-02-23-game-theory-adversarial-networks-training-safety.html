Good AI copies, great AI steals

Weekly: Thu, 23 Feb 2017

Featured

DeepMind's AI has learnt to become 'highly aggressive' when it feels like it's going to lose
wired.co.uk

Yes, it’s a click-bait-ey headline, but it’s a good one isn’t it? Filed in the “things that add to my confirmation bias that the machines are eventually coming for us”.

It’s worth noting the sidebar, that “In social situations, two AIs will also work together if the outcome benefits them both”. Sounds a bit like humans under the right conditions.

This is really an article about AI agents learning game-theory and applying optimal strategies in specific resource availability contexts. Not so many apples? Kill your competition. Lots of apples? Hey, lets work together.

This isn’t really surprising. But the subtle commentary on predictably bad outcomes given specific goal-orientations in a context of scarcity.. well, perhaps there’s a research paper in that too.
